KODIT "Little Penguin Program" mBaaS Selected

Attachment 1. Participation photo of ESG leading companies

mBaaS, a start-up company for students who moved into the Busan Center for the Korea Scholarship Foundation (hereinafter referred to as Busan Scholarship Foundation Dormitory), was selected for the Little Penguin program organized by the Korea Credit Guarantee Fund and secured 1 billion won in funds. The Little Penguin Program is the Korea Credit Guarantee Fund's flagship startup support program that selects promising startups with innovative technologies and creative ideas With the funds earned by the selection, Mvas plans to further upgrade its current automated app generation platform, AiApp, and strengthen its marketing strategy.
The AiApp provided by mBaaS is a platform created using LLM-based AI and mBaaS technology, and anyone can easily create an app without development knowledge. In fact, when you access the AiApp website (https://www.aiapp.help), you can select designs and functions, and this is how the app is created with the data selected by the user. The technology is efficient for both users and developers, and features low technical barriers that make it easy for anyone to develop apps.

Kim Jung-hyun, CEO of Mbas, is striving for shared growth with residents by engaging in various activities such as mentoring start-up support and networking, a dormitory program of the Busan Scholarship Foundation. This is because CEO Kim Jung-hyun's Mbas operation policy, which is usually interested in ESG management and social issues, aims to contribute to society beyond simply providing technical services.
mBaaS plans to grow beyond just app automatic creation/management platform services to become a platform that allows everyone to realize technology with ideas, optimized for individuals and businesses.
